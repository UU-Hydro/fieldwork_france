Hi Edwin,

I have would like to have some more edits to the Erosion model, maybe you can add those in your version to keep that all together nicely at github.

- for the two equations we changed to m (rather than mm) remove the 0.001 factor at the end, to get more realistic values

- input a max limit for flowdetachment @999 kg-m2-year (we can debate if this is useful, but does help for visualizing and sort of caps very extreme values that occur for the rivers.)

> this makes for quite realistic results in my opinion, for little vegetation and low ksat it gives ~200 ton/ha at the lower parts of steep areas which is nicely above the 50 ton/ha limit for no use to agriculture in the FAO table, but still within reasonable range.

for the reporting:

I would remove these static input maps from reports as they are direct inputs (maybe just comment the report statements out, if the students like they can add them back in):

vegetationCover
leafAreaIndex
plantHeight
canopyCover
groundCover
factorC
cohesion
erodibilityK

probably leave Ksat, and rather include these intermediate results:

canopyInterceptionCapacity

effectiveRainfallYear

leafDrainageYear

directThroughfallYear

maybe we can include runoff generated (just on a by pixel basis, that shows the excess rainfall before the whole accuflux), I am not sure if it is currently calculated
